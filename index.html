Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Prop Firm?

A Prop Firm, also known as a Proprietary Trading Firm, is a company that provides traders with trading money in exchange for a portion of the profits made by their trades.

These firms often employ a profit-sharing scheme in which the trader receives a percentage of the profits generated while the firm also earns a commission.

This method allows traders to gain access to large quantities of capital without having to invest their own money.

What is a prop firm funded account?

A prop firm funded account is a trading account offered to a trader by a proprietary trading firm. This account is funded by the prop firm's own funds, and the trader has access to it to trade.

 

Traders who do not have enough funds to trade on their own or who wish to access larger sums of capital to boost their trading positions frequently use the prop company funded account. The trader agrees to split a percentage of the profits made by their trades with the firm in exchange for access to the firm's capital.


Traders that are approved for a prop company funded account must often go through a rigorous assessment procedure to verify their trading skills and knowledge.

They are also expected to observe the prop firm's trading guidelines and risk management rules.

Why should I get funded?

A trader may consider becoming funded by a prop firm for a variety of reasons, including:

Access to Capital: Prop firms give traders access to large sums of capital. This enables traders to take larger positions and perhaps earn larger profits than they could with their own capital.

 

Reduced Risk: Because the capital provided by a prop firm is not the trader's own money, trading is less risky. This is especially useful for traders who do not have a lot of money to invest or who are new to trading.

 

Resources and Support: Prop firms provide traders with resources like as trading platforms, data feeds, and other tools that can assist them in making informed trading decisions. Furthermore, prop businesses frequently provide training, mentoring, and assistance to help traders develop their skills and become more successful.

 

Profit-Sharing: Most prop firms use a profit-sharing model, in which the trader keeps a percentage of the profits generated while the firm receives a commission. This structure can be advantageous for traders because it incentivizes them to perform well and pays them for their earnings.

 

Trading Discipline: Prop businesses frequently impose tight trading standards and risk management requirements on their traders. This can assist traders in developing discipline and excellent trading habits, which can be advantageous.
